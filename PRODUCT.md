# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Primary: hiring committees evaluating faculty or postdoc candidates. Scanning for research identity, output quality, and intellectual character. Forming first impressions fast — often from a shared link or Google Scholar profile click.

Secondary: researchers and potential collaborators assessing fit and reading recent work. Occasionally students.

Context: mostly desktop, often arriving with a name already in mind, not browsing cold.

## Product Purpose

Personal academic portfolio for Kha Dinh Duy, Postdoctoral Researcher at UBC Systopia Lab. Research spans OS design and software security — compartmentalization, side-channel defenses, automated program analysis.

Success: a visitor forms a clear, accurate picture of who Kha is as a researcher within 60 seconds. The site feels like the work — principled, careful, not trying too hard.

## Brand Personality

Precise, rigorous, personal, approachable.

The site should feel like it was made by a systems researcher — someone who thinks carefully about tradeoffs, values clarity over decoration, but has a personality behind the code. Not sterile. Not performative.

## Anti-references

- Generic al-folio clones: white/beige background, teal or cyan accent, uniform card grids, zero personality
- Flashy portfolio sites: hero animations, gradient text, floating elements, scroll-driven theatrics
- Corporate/LinkedIn register: achievement-bullet formatting, headshot-in-suit energy, blue-and-white professionalism
- Over-designed academic pages: visual novelty that competes with the research content

## Design Principles

1. **Content is the credential.** Publication titles, awards, and research descriptions do the persuading. Design frames them without competing.
2. **Earn every element.** Nothing decorative that isn't also structural. Restraint is a signal of taste, not timidity.
3. **Technical precision as aesthetic.** Monospace, tight spacing, deliberate hierarchy — the site should feel like it was built by someone who cares about the details of systems.
4. **Personal without being casual.** A hiring committee should sense a person, not a template. Small moments of character are welcome; nothing that undermines seriousness.
5. **No generic defaults.** Every choice that was left at the theme default is a missed opportunity to say something specific.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Support both light and dark mode (already present in theme). No motion that can't be suppressed. Sufficient contrast at both theme variants.
